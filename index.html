The Investment Case
Why Argentine Farmland. Why Now.

Argentina's Pampean belt is one of the world's most productive agricultural regions, consistently ranking among the top global exporters of soy, corn, and wheat. Yet farmland values remain at a significant discount to comparable international benchmarks — a structural mispricing created by years of macro distortion and policy uncertainty that is now beginning to normalize.

The FAA I fund captures this opportunity through a dual-return strategy: immediate cash yield from long-term lease contracts with established agricultural operators, and capital appreciation as land values reprice toward global comparables over the fund's 10-year horizon.

🤝 Acquisition Strategy — Two Pathways

FAA I acquires farmland through two complementary channels, giving sellers flexible exit options while expanding the fund's sourcing universe.

💵 Pathway 1 — Cash Purchase
Direct acquisition with fresh capital. Targets motivated sellers, off-market transactions and estates seeking full liquidity — at valuations favorable to the fund.
🔄 Pathway 2 — Land for Shares
Landowners exchange farmland for FAA I cuotapartes — a BYMA-listed, dividend-paying liquid asset. May qualify for capital gains tax exemption.

The Structural Opportunity

Prime U.S. farmland trades at USD 42,000/ha. Brazilian farmland at USD 27,500/ha. Argentine Zona Núcleo — equally productive, superior soils — currently sits at USD 17,000/ha: a 38% discount to Brazil and a 60% discount to the United States. This mispricing was created by decades of macro distortion and policy headwinds that are now actively reversing. FAA I is positioned to capture both the yield and the repricing.

Argentina Agriculture: The Investment Case

In his presentation at the Córdoba Stock Exchange (April 2025), the CEO of Argentina's largest publicly listed agricultural company — managing over 866,000 ha — presented three central arguments that directly support the FAA I investment thesis.

Agricultural Subsidies Worldwide — Argentina is the Outlier

According to OECD data (2022), Argentina is the only major agricultural producer applying negative subsidies to its farming sector — export taxes that artificially compress producer profitability and land values. While the EU, U.S., Brazil and Australia positively subsidize their farmers, Argentina taxes them.

Relevance for FAA I

Any normalization — reduction or elimination of export taxes — translates directly into higher tenant profitability and higher land values. Reductions already implemented (soy: 33%→26%, corn/wheat: 12%→9.5%) are a direct appreciation catalyst.

Public-private incentives for railways and ports — reduces logistics costs and increases net field income.

Tax incentives to stimulate legal purchases and increase productivity per hectare.

How We Invest
Asset Selection & Portfolio Construction

✓ Eligibility Criteria

  • Pampean belt: Buenos Aires, Córdoba, Santa Fe, Entre Ríos, La Pampa
  • 500–10,000 ha per parcel · INTA Class I, II or III soils
  • Apt for: soy, corn, wheat, sunflower, sorghum
  • Clear title · No disputes or possession conflicts
  • Basic infrastructure: vehicular access, storage capacity

🔍 Due Diligence Process

  • Dual independent appraisal (Tribunal de Tasaciones registered)
  • Full title study, domain report and encumbrance verification
  • Agronomy audit by independent Agricultural Auditor
  • Soil quality, productivity and water access analysis
  • Environmental and regulatory compliance check

📋 Lease Contract Standards

  • 3–5 year terms with established agricultural operators
  • Rents denominated in USD / corn-equivalent
  • Minimum 6-month rent guarantee (real or personal security)
  • Rent indexed to international commodity prices
  • Tenant responsible for environmental damage and soil erosion
  • Notarized and registered with property registry

⚖️ Portfolio Rules

  • Min. 75% of NAV in farmland assets at all times
  • Max. 30% LTV on acquisition financing (rural mortgage)
  • Max. 5% in derivative instruments (hedging only)
  • No direct farming operations · No crop production risk
